Step 02 · Experimental condition

Configure your manipulation.

Enable one or more manipulations and choose levels. The exact configuration is recorded with the data, so every participant in your group should use identical settings.

Baseline: 500 ms

How long the dots are visible on each trial. Shorter durations leave less time for evidence to accumulate in motion-sensitive neurons.

ms
Baseline: 0 px (centred)

Offsets the dot field to the side of the central fixation cross. On each trial the side is chosen randomly (left or right) to control for response biases. Use a ruler to convert the pixel value to a real visual angle if you wish.

px offset
Baseline: 2.5 px/frame

How fast each dot moves per display frame. Motion-sensitive neurons in MT are tuned to particular speeds.

px/frame
Baseline: 100 dots

The total number of dots in the aperture. More dots provide more samples of motion information for the visual system to pool.

dots
Baseline: 200 px radius

The radius of the circular region in which dots are visible.

px radius
Baseline: 100%

The luminance of the dots relative to the background. At 100% the dots are full white; at lower values they become progressively darker grey.

%

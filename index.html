What you will be doing

Across many short trials, a cloud of moving dots will appear briefly. On every trial some proportion of dots move coherently to the left or right; the rest move in independent random directions. After the dots disappear, the participant indicates the direction of coherent motion using the left and right arrow keys.

The proportion of coherently moving dots — the coherence — varies from trial to trial across six levels. At very low coherence the task is impossible; at high coherence it is trivial. Somewhere in between sits a coherence threshold: the level at which the participant responds correctly 75% of the time. You will use the data produced by this site to estimate that threshold and to compare it between conditions.

Before you start Use a laptop or desktop computer (not a phone). Sit roughly an arm's length from the screen, in a normally lit room. Close other tabs and applications. Each session takes around six to seven minutes.
